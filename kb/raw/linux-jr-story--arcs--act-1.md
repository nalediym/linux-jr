# Act 1 — Find & Look

**Theme:** The player arrives at the workshop as an ethical-hacker-in-
training. Pip and Captain Rex invite them in. Every mission is about
*finding* or *looking at* something — always with permission. No building
yet.

**Ethos beat:** "Look first. Ask first. Find kindly."

**Commands introduced:** `pwd`, `ls`, `cd`, `cat`, dotfiles (via `ls -a`
style but the game shows dotfiles by default — keep it simple).

**Emotional beat:** "I'm new here, but I can already find things."

**Badge earned:** **Scout** (observer who asks first)

---

## Mission 01 — The Missing Blueprint (existing, retrofit)

- **Location:** workshop (toolbox, kitchen, secret-room)
- **Problem:** Pip hid a robot-cat blueprint somewhere and forgot where
- **Character focus:** Pip
- **Commands (Track A):** pwd, ls, cd, cat, dotfiles
- **Concept (Track B):** *location* — "everything lives somewhere"
- **Vocabulary:** directory, file, path, hidden, flag
- **Hook:** "Have you ever lost something in your own room? Pip just did."
- **Real World Moment:** "When you tap 'Photos' on a phone, the phone is
  doing something like `ls` — it's asking, 'what's in this place?'"
- **Transfer question:** "Where do YOU keep things you want to find later?"
- **Canon preserved:** robot-cat = Sprocket's blueprint

**Retrofit notes:** Existing text already fits the Workshop world. Polish:
1. Cold-open line from Captain Rex log: "Captain Rex's log: Pip lost another
   one. Would you help?"
2. Concept-intro line from Pip: "Okay okay okay — first thing: every file has
   a place it lives. You find where you are by asking `pwd`."
3. Sprocket caption on first successful `ls`: "beep-boop!" → *ls = listing*
4. Real-world card after flag found, read by Captain Rex.
5. Name Sprocket explicitly in the payoff blueprint text.

## Mission 02 — The Secret Code (existing, retrofit)

- **Location:** workshop (garage/workbench, kitchen/pantry, study)
- **Problem:** Captain Rex forgot his own password (it happens!) and long
  ago left himself clues around the workshop in case this ever happened
- **Character focus:** Captain Rex
- **Commands (Track A):** ls, cd, cat across deeper directory tree
- **Concept (Track B):** *decomposition* — "break a big problem into small ones"
- **Vocabulary:** nested, deeper, clue, assemble
- **Hook:** "Have you ever tried to remember a phone number? You usually
  remember it in small chunks. That's what Captain Rex did with his password."
- **Real World Moment:** "Phone numbers, birthdays, even long words —
  your brain breaks big things into small pieces to remember them.
  That's called **decomposition**. Computers do it too."
- **Transfer question:** "What's a long thing you know (a song? a zip code?)
  that's actually made of smaller pieces?"
- **Canon preserved:** 2-2-4-2 combo (bike wheels, bird wings, dog legs, eyes)

**Retrofit notes (ethical-hacker reframe):** The old framing was "someone
locked Captain Rex's computer" — that reads like unauthorized access.
Reframe to: "Captain Rex forgot his own password. He set up clues around
the workshop for himself ages ago, just in case. He's asking for your
help putting them together." This makes the mission **explicitly
consensual** (Rex asks for help on his own system) and models the
ethos's "help, never harm" principle — the player is recovering access
for the rightful owner. Mission story draft:

> Captain Rex's log: "I did the thing. I forgot my own password. I left
> clues around the workshop years ago for exactly this. Would you help me
> find them all?"

## Mission 03 — The Maze (existing, retrofit)

- **Location:** basement (maze of old shelving)
- **Problem:** Pip went exploring and got turned around
- **Character focus:** Pip
- **Commands (Track A):** nested cd, cd .., dotfiles
- **Concept (Track B):** *sequencing* — "order matters: step by step"
- **Vocabulary:** parent (folder), back, nested, step
- **Hook:** "Have you ever given someone directions to your house?
  First this, then that, then that. That's what the notes in this maze do."
- **Real World Moment:** "Recipes, dance moves, even brushing your teeth —
  they're all **sequences**. One thing after another. That's what Pip's
  notes are: a sequence through the maze."
- **Transfer question:** "Tell me a sequence you do every morning."
- **Canon preserved:** follow notes, cookies at the end, Pip is found

**Retrofit notes:** Add Sprocket — "Pip took Sprocket down there. Sprocket's
been beeping from somewhere deep." Gives Act 1 a through-line of Sprocket.
Add Sprocket caption on Pip-find: "beep-beep-beep!" → *found it!*
