# The Workshop — Setting Bible

## One-line pitch

A cozy, slightly-messy tinker-workshop and home where Pip (a kid-inventor),
Captain Rex (a tinker-mentor), and Sprocket (a half-built robot cat) live
together and make things. The player is a visiting friend who drops by to
help.

## Vibe

- Roald Dahl's chocolate factory × a grandparent's garage × a Pixar short.
- Always warm lighting. Sawdust, cocoa, oil, cookies.
- Safe. Nothing is ever actually dangerous. Problems are always "oh dear,
  this is stuck/lost/tangled" — never "oh no, we're in trouble."
- Sprocket beeps in the background of most scenes.

## Locations (directories)

These are the rooms that already exist in missions 1–3, plus room to grow.
All filesystem trees in future missions should draw from this list.

### Canon (appear in M1–M3)

- **workshop/** — the main floor of the building. The "home" directory for
  most missions.
  - **toolbox/** — labeled drawers (some labels wrong, per Pip)
  - **kitchen/** — fridge with magnets + notes, pantry, cookie jar
  - **garage/** — bigger tools, workbench, oil cans
  - **garage/workbench/** — where Pip tinkers on Sprocket
  - **study/** — Captain Rex's quiet room. Computer, bookshelf,
    desk-drawer, worn rug.
  - **study/bookshelf/** — "rockets, dogs, Binary for Beginners"
  - **secret-room/** — a half-hidden side room behind a curtain. Pip's
    favorite hiding spot.
- **basement/** — downstairs. Cool, slightly dusty. Has a maze of old
  shelving units from when the workshop was a shop.
- **basement/entrance/** — the maze's start
- **basement/entrance/[left, right, down]/** — maze branches (see M3)

### Expansion (for missions 4–10 — pick from here, don't invent freely)

- **attic/** — up the ladder. Boxes of old inventions. Dust motes. Skylights.
- **roof/** — accessed from attic. Telescope (Captain Rex's secret hobby).
- **garden/** — out back. Greenhouse, shed, tomato patch, Sprocket's
  favorite sunny spot.
- **garden/shed/** — spare parts, old wagon wheels, paint cans.
- **garden/greenhouse/** — herb pots, a tomato vine that won't stop growing.
- **neighbor/** — the friendly neighbor's house next door. Sometimes Pip's
  inventions roll over there. (Can introduce a guest character later if needed.)
- **mailbox/** — at the front gate. Letters and packages come here.

### Never exists (don't invent)

- No basement sub-levels past the maze
- No second floor (just attic ladder from garage)
- No off-world locations (space, underwater, etc.) — this is grounded
- No dangerous places (cliff, dark tunnel, haunted room)

## Time

- Always a warm afternoon or early evening. No night scenes, no storms.
- "Tomorrow" and "yesterday" exist but the calendar is fuzzy. Kids don't need it.

## Recurring props

- **Pip's toolbox** — opens in at least one mission per act
- **Captain Rex's logbook** — on his desk in the study
- **Sprocket's tail** — detaches too easily; may need to be found
- **The cookie jar** — always in the kitchen, always has cookies
- **The hat Captain Rex wears** — his kid made it, never specified which kid
- **A red button** that does something surprising (to be revealed in Act 3)

## Things the world does NOT have

- Smartphones (Captain Rex still uses a desktop; messages are on paper or sticky notes)
- TV (Pip is too busy inventing)
- The internet in any form (this keeps the terminal metaphor clean)
- Any adults other than Captain Rex (the neighbor is implied, may appear as
  voice-only)

## Why this shape

Small enough to feel cozy. Enough rooms to host 10 missions without
re-using directory layouts. All locations are "places a kid could draw a
map of" — concrete, sensory, filesystem-friendly.
