## Definition
Four recurring, age-7-legible concepts that turn memorized keystrokes into transferable knowledge: decomposition, pattern matching, abstraction, sequencing.
— [educational.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--educational.md.md)#c-f0397d39, [story.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--story.yaml.md)#c-b795fdab

## Details
Kid-friendly glosses:
- **Decomposition** — break a big problem into small steps (M2 Secret Code).
- **Pattern matching** — "this looks like something I've seen before."
- **Abstraction** — a folder is an idea, it holds things (M4 Room for Sprocket).
- **Sequencing** — order matters: look before you act (M3 The Maze).
— [educational.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--educational.md.md)#c-f0397d39, [act-1.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-1.md.md)#c-512c914e, c-b5d8cbe1, [act-2.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-2.md.md)#c-760e4322

Act 3 extends the roster with *search strategy* (M7), *assembly/algorithms* (M8), *organization/design* (M9), and *teaching is knowing* (M10) — introduced as additional CT concepts beyond the original four.
— [act-3.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-3.md.md)#c-fbda42d5, c-e2b82257, c-0ae70827, c-b1518c4a
