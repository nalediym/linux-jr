# Cyberchase-Inspired Pedagogy

> **First seen in:** [story.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--story.yaml.md)
> **Also referenced by:** [educational.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--educational.md.md), [sprocket.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--characters--sprocket.yaml.md)
> **Confidence:** high (3 sources agree)
> **Aliases:** the pedagogy, six-beat mission shape

## Definition
An explicit-goal, real-world-callback, logic-puzzle teaching structure borrowed from Cyberchase (PBS). Every mission opens with a real-world hook a kid already relates to, then ties the command back to that real-world moment at the end.
— [educational.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--educational.md.md)#c-20f806d3, [story.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--story.yaml.md)#c-b795fdab

## Details
Two tracks in every mission: Track A (real Linux commands — the heart) and Track B (computational thinking — the understanding). Track B is scaffolding around Track A, not a replacement for it.
— [educational.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--educational.md.md)#c-560b8acf

Mission shape is always six beats: Hook → Mission Brief (Rex, with permission) → Concept Intro (Pip, kid-speak) → The Adventure → The Real World Moment (post-completion callback card) → Ethos Reflection (Rex, warm).
— [educational.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--educational.md.md)#c-20f806d3

Sprocket plays the Digit-inspired fact-beeper role: beeps are always captioned with a one-line concept reinforcement, max 2–3 per mission.
— [educational.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--educational.md.md)#c-52969373, [sprocket.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--characters--sprocket.yaml.md)#c-01786ca3, [story.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--story.yaml.md)#c-b795fdab

The ethos gives the values spine; Cyberchase gives the pedagogy spine. Together they produce a kid who thinks computationally, acts ethically, and speaks fluent command-line.
— [educational.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--educational.md.md)#c-51271b5c
