## Definition
The player's in-world identity: an ethical hacker-in-training who is really curious, really clever, and really kind. Key word: *help, not harm.*
— [story.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--story.yaml.md)#c-d7f97e6c, [ethos.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--ethos.md.md)#c-b8571851

## Details
Five kid-legible principles govern every interaction:
1. Ask first.
2. Help, never harm.
3. Look before you act (`ls`/`cat` before `mkdir`/`echo`).
4. Say what you did.
5. Curiosity is a superpower — when you use it kindly.
— [story.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--story.yaml.md)#c-d7f97e6c, [ethos.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--ethos.md.md)#c-0ca17ab7

The ethos shows up in gameplay through four mechanisms: invitation-style mission briefings, rare filesystem boundaries (`.private` dirs), after-action acknowledgements, and Pip occasionally forgetting to ask (max once per act) with a warm Rex correction.
— [ethos.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--ethos.md.md)#c-d6b03723, c-4399c4bd, [pip.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--characters--pip.yaml.md)#c-87175af9, [captain-rex.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--characters--captain-rex.yaml.md)#c-f353ab69

Style rules carry the ethos into every line of copy: briefings never say "break into," only "asked for help." Narrator acknowledges every mutation.
— [style.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--style.md.md)#c-89e93c78

M10 is explicitly the ethos capstone — every principle gets surfaced when the player becomes the guide for Juno.
— [act-3.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-3.md.md)#c-b1518c4a

Without the ethos, Linux Jr is a cute tutorial. With it, it's a kid's first lesson in responsible power.
— [ethos.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--ethos.md.md)#c-798fb852
