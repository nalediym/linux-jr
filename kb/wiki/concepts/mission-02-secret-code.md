## Definition
The second mission. Captain Rex forgot his own password and long ago left himself 4 digit clues around the workshop. The player assembles them — the answer is **2-2-4-2** (bike wheels, bird wings, dog legs, eyes).
— [act-1.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-1.md.md)#c-512c914e, [mission-02](../sources/src--data--missions--mission-02-the-secret-code.js.md)#c-102a9614

## Details
- **Character focus:** Captain Rex.
- **Track A commands:** reinforces ls/cd/cat across a deeper directory tree. No new command.
- **Track B concept:** *decomposition* — "break a big problem into small ones."
- **Vocabulary:** nested, deeper, clue, assemble.
— [act-1.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-1.md.md)#c-512c914e, [commands.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--commands.md.md)#c-c07ae2a4

**Ethos reframe (important):** the old framing was "someone locked Captain Rex's computer" — that read like unauthorized access. The reframe is "Rex forgot his own password and is asking for your help." The mission is now explicitly consensual.
— [act-1.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-1.md.md)#c-512c914e

The implementation already reflects the reframe: the in-code `story` string is verbatim "Captain Rex's log: I did the thing. I forgot my own password…" The 4 digits split 2 in the garage (toolbox + workbench) and 2 in the kitchen (fridge-note + pantry back-shelf). Study has the computer asking for the code.
— [mission-02](../sources/src--data--missions--mission-02-the-secret-code.js.md)#c-102a9614

Study props match Rex's character canon: bookshelf "rockets, dogs, Binary for Beginners"; desk drawer with a photo of Pip and a friend at the beach.
— [captain-rex.yaml](../sources/linux-jr-story--characters--captain-rex.yaml.md)#c-49366ca0
