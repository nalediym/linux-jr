# No-Tech World

> **First seen in:** [setting.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--setting.md.md)
> **Also referenced by:** [act-3.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-3.md.md)
> **Confidence:** high (2 sources agree)

## Definition
An intentional world-building constraint: the Linux Jr setting has no smartphones, no TV, no internet in any form, and no adults other than Captain Rex.
— [setting.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--setting.md.md)#c-89100828

## Details
Rex still uses a desktop. Pip is too busy inventing to watch TV. Messages are on paper or sticky notes.
— [setting.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--setting.md.md)#c-89100828

**Why this matters for the pedagogy:** removing the internet keeps the terminal metaphor clean. `cd` goes to a *real* room; there's nothing remote.
— [setting.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--world--setting.md.md)#c-89100828

This constraint is why act-3.md flags "network metaphors" as explicitly off-limits for future missions — they'd violate the no-internet rule.
— [act-3.md](../sources/linux-jr-story--arcs--act-3.md.md)#c-67be01e9
