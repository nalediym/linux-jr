## Chunks

### c-77916829: heart
> "Real Linux commands, typed by a real kid." By end of Season 1 kid can open a real Terminal on a real Mac and type pwd, ls, cd, cat, mkdir, touch, echo confidently. Never compromise: commands are real, typing is mandatory, kid graduates to a real Terminal, no gamification at the cost of muscle memory. Heart wins all tiebreakers.

### c-d7f97e6c: ethos
> Identity is ethical hacker-in-training. Principles: ask first, help never harm, look before you act (ls/cat before mkdir/echo), say what you did, curiosity is a superpower when used kindly.

### c-ffb86017: premise
> A kid learns to use real Linux commands — the same ones professional engineers type every day — inside a cozy tinker-workshop where they help Pip, Captain Rex, and Sprocket. Ethics shapes how they use the commands. The commands themselves are the heart.

### c-eef3c97b: tone
> Anchor: Nick Jr. / Bluey / Octonauts / Daniel Tiger. Register: preschool-friendly, ages 2–7 (gameplay scales up to 10+). Feelings cozy/curious/silly/warm. Never scary/mean/violent/dangerous.

### c-8f8e1e49: cast
> Pip (kid-inventor friend), Captain Rex (tinker-mentor, owner of the workshop), Sprocket (Pip's half-built robot-cat sidekick).

### c-aeda3b50: scope
> 10 missions across 3 acts. Act 1 Find & Look (M1–3: pwd, ls, cd, cat, dotfiles). Act 2 Make & Fix (M4–6: mkdir, touch, echo, simple pipes?). Act 3 Combine (M7–10).

### c-d20b593c: endings / badges
> Act 1: Scout badge ("you find things and ask before acting"). Act 2: Helper badge ("you build and fix — always with permission"). Act 3: White Hat badge ("you can do it all, and you do it right").

### c-5366e51f: mechanic
> Typed-command tutorial. No damage. No fail states. Hints always available via `.hint` files in every room.

### c-b795fdab: education
> Cyberchase-inspired pedagogy. Two tracks: Linux commands (surface) + computational thinking (decomposition, pattern matching, abstraction, sequencing). Six-beat mission shape. Sprocket is the fact-beeper (Digit-inspired).

### c-d8c1c07c: setting
> The Workshop — cozy standalone tinker-workshop/home. Occupants Pip, Captain Rex, Sprocket. Player role visiting friend who comes by to help.

### c-d9dfbe60: style
> Simple, short sentences (1–3 per story block). Never say: wrong, error, failed, bad, stupid. Always say: try, let's see, got it, nice work, hmm. Signature phrases: Pip "Oh! Oh! Watch this!", Rex "Let's have a look.", Sprocket "Beep-boop!".
