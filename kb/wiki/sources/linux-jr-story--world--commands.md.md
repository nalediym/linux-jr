## Chunks

### c-c6ef427f: The promise
> By end of Season 1 (10 missions), the kid can confidently use `pwd`, `ls`, `cd`, `cd ..`, `cat`, `mkdir`, `touch`, `echo "x" > file`, `echo "x" >> file`, `help`/`man` in a real shell. Plus the concept of dotfiles and filesystem tree shape. A real, foundational Linux vocabulary — more than many adults have.

### c-c07ae2a4: The command ladder
> Every mission introduces OR reinforces commands; no mission is decorative. M1 introduces pwd/ls/cd/cat + dotfiles. M2 reinforces ls/cd/cat across deeper trees. M3 introduces cd ... M4 mkdir. M5 touch. M6 echo >. M7 combo breadth. M8 echo >>. M9 help/man. M10 capstone.

### c-c97c193b: Why "real" matters
> Lots of kid-coding tools use pretend languages (Scratch blocks, drag-and-drop, fake robot-control DSLs) — they teach logic but do NOT build muscle memory. Linux Jr solves this: a kid who completes 10 missions has already typed `ls` hundreds of times and doesn't have to unlearn anything later.

### c-6efe10b6: Design decisions "real" constrains
> Real filesystem semantics (files are files, directories are directories, `..` means parent). Errors are real errors with kid-friendly wording. Task checks are behavioral not typed-exact. Tab completion works. Autocorrect/autocapitalize OFF on iPad. Unknown commands fail honestly ("We don't use that one yet").

### c-f1f3317e: Never-compromise commitments
> 1. Commands are real. 2. Typing is mandatory (no click-to-execute). 3. The player graduates — by M10 kid can open a real Terminal and navigate their own Mac. 4. No gamified shortcuts at the cost of muscle memory.

### c-55674787: The three layers hierarchy
> HEART (real Linux commands) → HOW WE USE (ethical-hacker ethos) → HOW WE TEACH (Cyberchase pedagogy) → WHERE (workshop + Pip/Rex/Sprocket). If any layer conflicts with "real commands taught by real typing," the heart wins.

### c-61a9e221: Graduation moment / inspiration
> Real-world parallels: Duolingo (real language, not fake). Piano lessons (real piano, not toy). Bike with training wheels (real bike). The test of graduation: at end of Season 1 kid opens Terminal.app on a Mac, types `pwd`, smiles. That is the whole product succeeding.
