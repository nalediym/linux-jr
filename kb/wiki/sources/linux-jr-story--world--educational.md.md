## Chunks

### c-560b8acf: Two learning tracks
> Track A: Real Linux commands (the heart — non-negotiable). Track B: Computational thinking (the understanding). Track A is the heart; Track B is scaffolding around it.

### c-f0397d39: Computational thinking — four concepts
> Decomposition (break big problems into small). Pattern matching ("this looks like something I've seen"). Abstraction (a folder is an idea, it holds things). Sequencing (order matters).

### c-20f806d3: Mission shape (6 beats)
> 1. Hook (15s real-world relatable). 2. Mission Brief (Captain Rex, with permission). 3. Concept Intro (Pip, 1–2 sentences, kid-speak). 4. The Adventure (the puzzle). 5. The Real World Moment (post-completion card). 6. Ethos Reflection (Rex, warm). Borrowed directly from Cyberchase.

### c-8630f198: Vocabulary ladder (10 missions)
> Act 1: directory, file, path, hidden, flag, root, parent (folder), listing. Act 2: create, empty file, write, contents, marker, structure. Act 3: search, organize, combine, project, command, help.

### c-ca588740: Never-used words (age 7)
> Never say: syscall, kernel, shell, stdin/stdout, exit code, pipe (except as metaphor in later seasons), permissions (deferred to Season 2), process.

### c-52969373: Sprocket as fact-beeper (Digit role)
> Digit in Cyberchase narrates facts. Sprocket gets that role in Linux Jr, translated through beeps + on-screen captions (e.g. "beep-boop!" → "pwd = print working directory"). Max 2–3 Sprocket-caption moments per mission.

### c-51271b5c: Why blend ethical-hacker + Cyberchase
> Ethical hacker gives values spine (ask, help, transparency). Cyberchase gives pedagogy spine (explicit goals, real-world callbacks, logic puzzles). Together: a kid who thinks computationally, acts ethically, speaks fluent command-line.
