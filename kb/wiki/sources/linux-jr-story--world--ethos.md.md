## Chunks

### c-b8571851: Ethical hacker identity
> An ethical hacker is really curious, really clever, really kind. Key word: help, not harm. Looks carefully, asks permission, fixes what's broken, leaves notes about what they changed, never peeks where they weren't invited.

### c-0ca17ab7: The five principles (kid-legible)
> 1. Ask first. 2. Help, never harm. 3. Look before you act (`ls` and `cat` come before `mkdir` and `echo`). 4. Say what you did. 5. Curiosity is a superpower when used kindly.

### c-d6b03723: Ethos in gameplay
> Mission briefings start with an explicit invitation from Rex or Pip. Filesystem boundaries: `.private` directories with `.hint` saying "not for us today" (use sparingly, once or twice across 10 missions). After-action transparency: narrator acknowledges every mutation ("Nice — you built Sprocket a bed here.").

### c-4399c4bd: When Pip slips up (teachable moment)
> Pip sometimes forgets to ask first — max once per act. Example: Pip opens Rex's logbook without asking; Rex gently says "Mm — you could have asked, you know." Pip says "Oh — sorry, Rex." Not a punishment beat, a modeling beat.

### c-2c3eaa21: Badges (renamed)
> Act 1 Scout (finds things, observes, asks before acting). Act 2 Helper (builds and fixes, always with permission). Act 3 White Hat (the full ethical hacker). Previous draft was Explorer / Tinker / Workshop Key — renamed so every badge reinforces the ethos.

### c-798fb852: Why this reframe matters
> Without the ethos, Linux Jr is a cute tutorial. With it, it's a kid's first lesson in responsible power. Every season can teach a new layer (consent → debugging → honest reporting → teaching others).
