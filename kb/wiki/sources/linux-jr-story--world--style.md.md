## Chunks

### c-c0c6d8e6: Voice
> Short sentences, 1–3 per story block. Concrete nouns ("cookies", "bolt", "tail", "toolbox"). Active verbs ("Pip hid", "Sprocket beeped"). Present tense for descriptions, past for what just happened.

### c-ae0d5641: Never say
> "wrong", "error", "failed", "bad", "stupid", "dangerous", "scary", "nightmare", "terrible", "hate", "shut up", "dumb", "loser", "weird" (as insult).

### c-c56d105d: Always say (when the kid misfires)
> "Hmm, I don't know that one yet." (existing canon.) "Let's try another way." "Not quite — let's have another look." "Almost!"

### c-d622fd21: Signature phrases
> Pip: "Oh! Oh! Watch this!" (starting something new), "Okay okay okay —" (thinking aloud). Captain Rex: "Let's have a look." (briefing), "Well now." (surprised), "Mm." (thinking). Sprocket: "Beep-boop!", "Beep." (yes/hi), "Beep-beep-beep!" (excited/found it).

### c-17a067c0: Mission story field — the 1–3 sentence rule
> Shape: [one line world state] / [one line problem] / [optional one line call to action]. Read aloud by TTS and shown on screen.

### c-89e93c78: Ethical-hacker voice in copy
> Briefings always start with an invitation (never "break into", always "asked for help"). After state-changing actions, acknowledge what the kid did. Boundary files (rare). Pip slipping up is modeled warmly, max once per act. Vocabulary: "Did you ask first?", "May I?", "Say what you did", "We fix. We don't break."

## Key Claims
- Story text is 1–3 sentences per block with a fixed shape. — c-c0c6d8e6, c-17a067c0
- There is an explicit banned word list. — c-ae0d5641
- Each character has signature phrases that carry voice. — c-d622fd21
- Kid misfires use encouraging language, never "wrong" or "error". — c-c56d105d
- Mission briefings are always invitations, not commands. — c-89e93c78
